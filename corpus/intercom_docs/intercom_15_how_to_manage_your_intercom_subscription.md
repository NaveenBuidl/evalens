### Managing seats

To add or remove seats from your subscription, you'll need to go to [Settings > Workspace > Teammates](https://app.intercom.com/a/apps/_/settings/workspace/teammates) and add a teammate which adds a seat, or remove a teammate which removes a seat (unless it's contracted - [see below)](https://www.intercom.com/help/en/articles/8344189-how-to-manage-your-intercom-subscription#h_2effebaab0). When removing a teammate:

If seats are removed during the billing cycle, credits for the unused time will be applied to your next invoice. However, existing invoices won't reflect these reductions; rather, adjustments will appear in the following billing period. Keep in mind that seat adjustments are typically not allowed mid-term for fixed plans / contracts. For self-serve subscription models, you can manage seat counts directly in your subscription settings without contacting support, providing greater flexibility and control over your seat count and associated costs.

### Switching between full and lite seats

You can also change seat types (e.g., from full to lite seats) to optimize your subscription in [Settings > Workspace > Teammates](https://app.intercom.com/a/apps/_/settings/workspace/teammates). After adjusting your seats, you'll be able to confirm subscription details and billing changes.

## Essential Plan Constraints

The Essential Plan requires active teammates for all seats. To reduce unnecessary seats and costs, remove unneeded teammates before proceeding with any seat adjustments.

## Early Stage Plan Fixed Seat Bundles

Customers on the Early Stage Plan have fixed seat bundles (e.g., advanced seats and Copilot seats) that cannot be modified mid-term, though feature access permissions can be managed within `Settings > Workspace > Teammates`.

## Understanding Your Contracted Billing with Intercom

If you're an Intercom customer with a contract, your billing is made up of two main components: fixed charges and variable charges. Understanding the difference between these is key to managing your costs effectively.

### Fixed contract charges: your base plan

Your fixed charges cover the core of your Intercom subscription, as outlined in your contract.

These charges are for a set number of teammate seats and your base plan, which might include a specific number of Fin AI Agent resolutions or other usage limits.

### Variable charges for additional usage

Variable charges, also known as overage charges, are for any usage that goes beyond the limits set in your contract. These are billed separately from your fixed charges and are calculated based on your actual usage.

### How other usage-based charges work

Some plans have usage limits for features like Fin AI Agent resolutions. If you exceed this limit, you will incur overage charges.

### Restoring Access and Data

When transitioning between plans, for example, switching to the Essential Plan after cancelling a prior Advanced subscription, all workspace data will be restored upon resubscription.

## Special program considerations

Customers participating in discount programs, such as the Early Stage program, may face additional restrictions or require manual intervention for any changes to their subscription plan. Please contact our support team through the Messenger for assistance with these exceptions.
